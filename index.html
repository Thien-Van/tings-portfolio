Hi, I'm Ting

Based in Berlin, I am in the startup capital of Germany and naturally I have been working in some of them already. My passion is to create digital media and through my experience as a Game Designer I am always looking for ways to make user interaction fun and exciting.

About me

I am a big fan of games and online media. Every time I consume them, I am picking out features that I like and thinking about ways to improve the experience. The reason why I started the Thinkful program was to learn how to be able to actually implement these improvements and test my own ideas in real life. Outside of coding, gaming and designing I am a theme park enthusiast and a passionate cook.

I am actually in the process of creating a Youtube channel to publish my recipes and have some fun cooking on camera. In the future, after graduating from the Thinkful program I will be able to offer a full service package of creating and running a website or working on bigger projects as part of a team while my main focus would be front-end development.

Projects

Raycast

Raycast is a skill-based 3D first person game with a minimalistic art style. It was a student project, made by a team of six as a requirement for the third semester of my Game Design Bachelor with about 300h of time put into it. The target audiance for the game are adult PC players with an interst in athmospheric skill based experiences. My responsibilities were Game System Design, Level Design and Programming. I worked with Photoshop in the ideation phase and used the Unreal Development Kit to develop the game. We used Atlassian for the documentation and Asana as our project management tool. The programming was done with the UDK Kismet visual coding system.

Neon Lines

Neon Lines is a fast pace flow driven action game. It is based on a rythmic concept of movement. It was a student project, made by a team of six as a requirement for the fourth semester of my Game Design Bachelor with about 300h of time put into it. The target audiance for the game are adult PC players who enjoy rythmic gameplay and skill based combat with a colourful artstyle. My responsibilities were Producing, Game System Design and Level Design. I worked with Photoshop in the ideation phase and used the Unreal Engine 4 to develop the game. We used Atlassian for the documentation and Trello as our project management tool. In addition I used Audacity for the sound design.

Shock Tactics

Shock Tactics is a single player turn-based sci-fi strategy game with tactical combat, exploration, squad management and base building. This game was made by Point Blank Games where I started as a Game Desing intern and ended up as a Junior Game Designer. Our target audiance were adult PC players with an interest in tactical turn-based combat. It was published by Eurovideo Medien and released on 11th April 2017. I worked with the Unity engine, Photoshop, Illustrator and we used Trello as our project management tool.
